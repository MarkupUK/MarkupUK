Geert Bormans

Geert Bormans is since long an angled bracket jack-of-all-trades. He loves the beauty of a well architected solution or a pure and simplified process. Geert makes a living as an independent consultant providing XML or Link Open Data related solutions, mainly in the publishing industry. He does so with a broad geographical flexibility.

Geert likes an occasional interesting challenge, often easily found when having two teenage daughters. However, he prefers the challenges to involve alpine ground, six strings or markup.

Ari Nordström

Ari Nordström is a freelance markup geek, based in Göteborg, Sweden, but offering his services across a number of borders. He has provided angled brackets to a number of organisations and companies over the years.

Ari is the proud owner and head projectionist of Western Sweden's last functioning 35/70mm cinema, situated in his garage, which should explain why he once wrote a paper on automating commercial cinemas using XML.

Andrew Sales

Andrew is old enough not to be able to forget SGML.

He began writing DTDs and Python programs for publishers in 2000 and, like a streaming processor, he hasn't looked back.

His day job involves architecting content for a London-based UK publisher. Out of hours, he helps organise XML conferences and maintains the Schematron international standard.
