Call for Papers Now Open!

This Year's Theme Is The Future of Distributed Markup Systems!

The events of the past year have impacted all our lives significantly, including the way we work. Against this backdrop, we might ask ourselves: what role have markup technologies played?

We'd like to hear about:

  • the challenges that confronted you under changed circumstances
  • what worked, what didn't work so well (and indeed, what stayed the same)
  • emerging trends in markup systems prompted by lockdowns and increased home-working, and how you see these developing in future.

We of course remain interested in other good-quality markup stories.

How to submit

We are using the Microsoft CMT system again this year for managing abstract submissions and peer review. Before submitting an abstract, please register as an author at https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/MARKUPUK2021/Submission/Index.

If you have any questions about submitting an abstract, about the kind of topic that might suit our audience, or about anything else to do with speaking at Markup UK, please don't hesitate to contact us. If this is your first time submitting an abstract, we'd particularly love to hear from you.

Submission process

At the moment we would like to see extended abstracts of 500 words or more - we will ask for full papers in DocBook 5 format for all accepted abstracts before the conference.

Paper Submission

The conference audience is a good mixture of markup geeks, academics, publishers, software engineers, and end users.

When you submit an abstract, it will be reviewed by our Programme Committee. If your abstract is accepted, we'll expect you to write it up as a full paper for inclusion in the conference proceedings and give a presentation at the conference itself.
